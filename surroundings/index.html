The wider setting

No town is an island — least of all a port one

Follonica sits at the head of its own gulf, wedged between the sea, the Maremma plain and the old mining hills. Its economy borrows from all three, and from a ring of neighbouring towns.

🌊

The Gulf of Follonica

A broad arc of shallow, sandy coast facing the island of Elba. Blue-flag beaches and a shielded bay make the sea the town's headline asset — and its brand.

🌲

The pineta & nature reserves

Coastal pinewoods and protected wetlands (part of the Bandite di Scarlino and nearby reserves) buffer the beach and pull walkers, cyclists and birdwatchers off-season.

🏔️

The Colline Metallifere

The “Metalliferous Hills” behind town — a UNESCO Global Geopark whose ore fed Follonica's foundries for centuries and now sells mining heritage as tourism.

The neighbourhood

Towns that share the economy

Follonica works as a hub: a rail stop, a beach base and a shopping town for a cluster of smaller centres, each with its own economic specialism.

Piombino steel & port

Down the coast, home to one of Italy's major steelworks and a ferry port to Elba. The heavy industry Follonica gave up still beats next door.

Massa Marittima heritage

A hilltop medieval town and former mining capital, with a celebrated cathedral and mining museums — a classic Follonica day trip.

Scarlino & Gavorrano nature & mines

Marinas, nature reserves and the Parco delle Colline Metallifere's mining parks knit tourism to the old extractive landscape.

Elba & the islands across the water

The Tuscan Archipelago frames every sunset from the beach and shares the same visitor flows through the Gulf.

One landscape, many incomes

How the surroundings pay Follonica back

The town's hinterland isn't scenery — it's supply chain and product all at once.

Food & wine

Maremma olive oil, Sangiovese-based wines, wild boar, honey and cheese give restaurants a story to sell and farms a second income through tastings.

Outdoor tourism

Trails, gravel-cycling routes and geopark itineraries spread visitors beyond the beach and across the calendar.

Industrial anchor

Piombino's port and steelworks keep well-paid industrial jobs within commuting range, cushioning the seasonal service economy.
