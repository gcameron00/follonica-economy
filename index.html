Follonica · Maremma · Tuscany

From cast iron to coastline.

Follonica is a town the sea rebuilt. Founded around a grand-ducal iron foundry, it traded furnaces for beach umbrellas and became one of the Gulf of Follonica's leading tourist economies. Here is how that economy works — in data and in history.

The shape of it

Three engines drive the local economy

Like much of coastal Tuscany, Follonica runs on services — but its history gives each layer a distinct character.

🏖️

The tertiary engine

Tourism, hospitality, retail and second homes. The summer season concentrates most of the year's spending into roughly twelve weeks — the town's great strength and its scheduling headache.

🛠️

The industrial memory

The foundries closed, but metalworking, boat-yards, construction and artisan food keep a secondary sector alive — and Piombino's steelworks loom just up the coast.

🌾

The land & sea

Maremma olive oil, wine, honey and small-scale fishing form a modest primary sector that increasingly sells itself as “experience”: agritourism, tastings and farm stays.

A little history

A company town that outlived its company

Follonica barely existed until iron gave it a reason to. Grand Duke Leopold II of Tuscany expanded the ironworks in the early 1800s; the town grew around the furnaces, complete with a church whose portico is cast in iron. When the foundries faded, the beach took over.
